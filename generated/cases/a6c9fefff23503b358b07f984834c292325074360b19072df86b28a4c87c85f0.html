The DIKW Hierarchy
The Data, Information, Knowledge, Wisdom (DIKW) Pyramid/Hierarchy
is a model used to illustrate how we move from raw signals to deep
understanding and action.
INFO20003 Database Systems 5
1. Data (The Raw Material)
Data is the base of the pyramid. It consists of raw facts, signals,
or symbols without any context or interpretation.
• Characteristics: Objective, discrete, and unorganized.
• Form: Numbers, characters, or observations.
• Question answered: "What?"
• Example: A sensor records the number "85". On its own, this is just a
value.
INFO20003 Database Systems 6
2. Information (The Context)
Information is data that has been cleaned, processed, and
given a specific meaning. You "inform" the data by adding
context.
• Characteristics: Organized, categorized, and useful.
• Form: Sentences, formulas, or formatted reports.
• Question answered: "Who, what, where, or when?"
• Example: You learn that "85" is the heart rate (BPM) of a patient named
Alex at 10:00 AM. Now the number has a purpose.
INFO20003 Database Systems 7
3. Knowledge (The Application)
Knowledge occurs when information is synthesized and
integrated with existing experience. It involves seeing patterns
or trends across different pieces of information.
• Characteristics: Practical, applicable, and actionable.
• Form: Procedures, expertise, or "know-how."
• Question answered: "How?"
• Example: You compare Alex's heart rate of 85 BPM to their resting
average of 60 BPM. You recognize that this is a significant elevation for
this specific individual.
INFO20003 Database Systems 8
4. Wisdom (The Judgement)
Wisdom sits at the very top. It is the ability to use knowledge to
make sound judgments and ethical decisions. While knowledge
is about "doing things right," wisdom is about "doing the right
things."
• Characteristics: Evaluative, long-term, and uniquely human.
• Form: Values, vision, and discernment.
• Question answered: "Why?"
• Example: Knowing the heart rate is high (Knowledge), you decide not to
administer a stimulant medication and instead call for a medical
assessment to ensure the patient's safety.
INFO20003 Database Systems 9
What is a relational database?
What is a relational database (DB)?
• A relational database is a structured collection of data (conceptually)
organized into tables with rows and columns, where relationships between
data are established using keys (primary and foreign keys).
• It ensures data integrity, reduces redundancy, and allows for efficient
querying using SQL (Structured Query Language).
• A Database Management System (DBMS) is a software system designed
to store, manage, and facilitate access to databases.

INFO20003 Database Systems 18
FEIT AI Use Policy
FEIT allows the use of Generative AI (GenAI) in open assessment tasks, unless the
assessment instructions include a specific "AI Use Statement" that limits or prohibits the use.
Using GenAI is not an academic integrity breach on its own. However, your submitted work
must still be your own, and you remain fully responsible for meeting all academic integrity
requirements – this includes properly acknowledging and referencing any GenAI you use. We
encourage all students to take shared responsibility for their learning, and consider potential
impacts on secure assessment tasks.
For this particular subject, we note that for all assessments AI use is not endorsed.
Although AI use could be meaningful for some subjects in some assessments, in this
subject, people should solve all assessments with their own effort. This is important as
your assessments are here in this subject not only to develop the understanding and
skills that are needed for your future usage of database technologies, but also as
preparation for the final exam. In the final exam, you will be doing similar problems on
your own without any external assistance.
If anything in this policy is unclear, please speak with your teaching team for guidance.
