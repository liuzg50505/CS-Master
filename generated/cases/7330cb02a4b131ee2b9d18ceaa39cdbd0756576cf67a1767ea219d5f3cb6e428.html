AGGREGATE FUNCTION
Aggregation function: summarizes statistical information from a group of
tuples into a single value.
 Built-in aggregation functions over attribute X, e.g., age, salary
 COUNT( * ): How many employees are working in Dept 5?
 SUM ( Salary ): Sum up all salaries of employees in Dept 5.
 MAX( Age ) / MIN ( Age ): Who is the youngest employee in Dept. 5?
 AVG( Salary ): Average salary of employees in Dept. 5
 CORR(Salary, Age): Correlation between Age and Salary of employees in
Dept. 5
 …
Note: NULL values are discarded apart from COUNT(*).
Note: Define our own function,
 e.g., calculate the GPA as a weighted sum of grades;
 e.g., calculate the Euclidean distance between two geo-locations in Location-based
Services applications…

ANALYTICS: GROUPING TUPLES
 Partition a relation into groups based on grouping attribute, i.e.,
grouping tuples together having the same value in the grouping
attribute.
GROUP BY {grouping attribute}
Which is the grouping attribute and the expected number of groups?
 1: Group of employees with the same last-name
 2: Group of employees working in the same department
 3: Group of dependents of the same employee
 4: Group of projects in the same location
 5: Group of employees with the same salary…
 Then, we apply aggregation functions to each group.

IN-CLASS EXAMPLE: GROUP BY
Show the number of employees per department & average salary per
department.
SELECT  DNO, COUNT (*), AVG (Salary)
FROM  EMPLOYEE
GROUP BY DNO;
Step 1: Partition EMPLOYEE into separate groups w.r.t. department.
Step 2: For each group, calculate its cardinality and average salary.
Note 1: The grouping attribute must appear in SELECT
Note 2: If the grouping attribute has NULL values, then a separate group is

[A2] WORKED EXAMPLE
Which is the average salary of employees per department? Include also the
department name at the results.
SELECT  D.DNAME, AVG (E.SALARY)
FROM  DEPARTMENT AS D, EMPLOYEE AS E
WHERE  D.DNUMBER = E.DNO
GROUP BY D.DNAME;
Step 1: Associate EMPLOYEE with DEPARTMENT
Step 2: Group associated tuples together w.r.t. DNAME (unique)
Step 3: For each group, take the average of salaries

GROUP BY & HAVING
HAVING: condition to select/reject an entire group after grouping!
Show the number of employees per project only from those projects with
more than 2 employees. Include also the project name at the results.
SELECT          P.PNAME, COUNT (*)
FROM          PROJECT AS P, WORKS_ON AS W
WHERE          P.PNUMBER = W.PNO
GROUP BY    P.PNAME
HAVING         COUNT(*) > 2
