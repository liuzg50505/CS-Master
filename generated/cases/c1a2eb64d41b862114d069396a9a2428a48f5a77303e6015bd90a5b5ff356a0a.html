Question: what shifts people’s attitudes on immigration?
Highly salient issue in the recent years
Competing theories:
Contact Hypothesis: Outgroup hostility may diminish when people from different
groups interact in meaningful ways.
Threat Hypothesis: Some individuals may perceive outgroup presence as a threat,
potentially increasing hostility.
Research question: Does contact with Spanish-speaking outgroup member
causally affect people’s attitudes toward immigration in Boston?
Enos, Ryan D. 2017. The Space between Us: Social Geography and Politics.
