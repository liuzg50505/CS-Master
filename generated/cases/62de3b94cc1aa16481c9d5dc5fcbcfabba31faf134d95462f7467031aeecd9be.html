1 Introduction
There is tremendous hype around neural networks, as the cornerstone of deep learning.
The purpose of these notes is to introduce one of the simplest architectures (feed-forward,
fully connected networks) in a piece-by-piece procedure that should hopefully make them
slightly less mysterious than they appear in different places.
There are many excellent textbooks and online resources for learning about neural
networks (for further resources see the references at the end of the notes).
1.1 Brief History
The history of neural networks, dates back to the 1940s; see Figure 1 for a rough timeline
along with milestones in the developments of deep learning. The original motivation for
feed-forward neural networks(also known asartificial neural networks) arose
from analogies in how real biological brains work. Neurons (nerve cells) transmit in-
formation in the brain through detailed and complex wirings that connect individual
neurons to other neurons, cells, or glands. A first approximation (known as theMcCul-
loch & Pittsmodel) assumes that each neuron fires depending on a combination of the
activity of its inputs and strength of its incoming connections. As we will see, this is
related to the form and assumptions underlying feed-forward neural networks. However,
the feed-forward neural networks used in practice today differ from biological brains in
many ways.
Neural networks rose to fame in 1980s. However, it was not until the 2010s that they
started to become very widely used, resurfacing with the name of deep learning, including
Figure 1: A rough history of neural networks and deep learning from Lones (2024). For
a more detailed history, see Schmidhuber (2014).

different model architectures, and big accomplishments in specific fields such as image
classification and text modeling. One of the first areas of breakthrough was the field
of automatic speech recognition (Dahl et al (2011)). However, the most attention came
when it was shown the deep convolutional neural networks could significantly improve
performance on challenging image classification benchmarks (Krizhevsky et al (2012)).
The “explosion” in the use of deep learning has several contributing factors, including
i) advances in computation and hardware and ii) the growing volume of large labelled
datasets. More recently, the storm of Large Language Models has furthered added to
the success, relying on specific model structures, calledtransformers(Vaswani et al

example which perceptrons (or logistic regression) cannot solve.
