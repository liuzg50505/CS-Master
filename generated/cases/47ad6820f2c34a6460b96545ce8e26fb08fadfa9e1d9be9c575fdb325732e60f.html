1. ASSIGNMENT OVERVIEW
This assignment is a small-group exploratory project where students work on ONE speech
processing task using existing tools, datasets, and libraries.
You will choose a speech-related problem (e.g. speech recognition, speaker diarization,
emotion detection, speech synthesis) and approach it in ONE of three ways:
• Analyse speech data to extract insight
• Build a working speech-based system
• Explore and compare speech processing tools or methods
You are NOT expected to develop new models. The emphasis is on understanding how
speech systems work, how speech information is represented, and what the results mean.
The assignment is designed to be completed within approximately three weeks.
2. HOW TO IDENTIFY YOUR CATEGORY
Step 1: Choose ONE speech task
Examples include:
• Speech recognition / transcription
• Speaker diarization
• Speaker verification or identification
• Speech emotion or sentiment analysis
• Speech synthesis
• Speech-based analytics or retrieval
Step 2: Choose ONE project approach (Category A, B, or C below)
Each group must select ONE primary category below. Hybrid topics are allowed.
Category A – Applied Speech Analytics (Analyse & Explain)
NHS USM
Focus: Understanding and analysing information extracted from speech.
What you do:
• Analyse speech data
• Explain patterns, errors, or relationships
• Focus on insight rather than deployment
Examples:
• Emotion or sentiment detection from speech
• Stress or affect analysis
• Error analysis of speech recognition output
• Relationship between acoustic features and meaning
• Speaker embedding analysis or clustering.
Category B – Speech Systems as Applications (Build & Demonstrate)
Focus: Building and demonstrating a working speech-based system.
What you do:
• Design an end-to-end pipeline
• Demonstrate input → processing → output
• Evaluate system behaviour
Examples:
• Speech-to-text system using Wav2Vec2 or Whisper
• Speech synthesis system using Bark
• Speaker diarization system for meetings
• Speaker verification system using speech embeddings
• Voice-enabled analytics or decision-support system
Category C – Comparative / Exploratory Speech Studies (Explore & Compare)
Focus: Understanding strengths and limitations of speech tools or representations.
What you do:
• Compare models, tools, or representations
• Analyse behaviour under different conditions
• Explain trade-offs and limitations
Examples:
• Comparing ASR systems (Whisper vs Wav2Vec2)
• Comparing speaker embeddings for diarization or verification
• Explainable analysis of speech features or embeddings
• Robustness analysis under noise or accent variation
NHS USM
3. TOOLS AND LIBRARIES
You are encouraged to use open-source speech processing tools, including:
• Hugging Face Transformers and Datasets (Wav2Vec2, Whisper, HuBERT)
• OpenAI Whisper
• Bark (speech synthesis)
• Librosa
• PyTorch or TensorFlow
Google Colab is strongly recommended for development and experimentation.

Each group must submit:

6. SUBMISSION
You will need to submit the report in softcopy format. There is no maximum or minimum
pages but it is advisable to limit to 15 pages to ensure the content is precise and succinct. This
is for the font mostly 11 or 12 pt Times New Roman or Calibri with 1.5 spacing.  If you are
following the scientific paper format (font 9pt or 10pt no -spacing Calibri) it will be half the
above length. The report needs to follow manuscript author, year format. You can choose
APA or Harvard or Chicago format for citation, just ensure you stick to one.
APA Citation Guide maybe followed from here: https://sfcollege.libguides.com/apa/articles
You are also required to submit the code  and the report must contain all relevant and the
original resource reference to give credit where it is due and the video. The code should be
submitted either in a .ipynb file , or, if it is standalone or not accessible from the Google
Colab platform, must include a sufficient explanation on how to make it runnable  in other's
machine. Place all files in a folder labelled  with your group number, and compress
everything (using zip or rar) before submitting it through the Assignment 2 Submission link.
