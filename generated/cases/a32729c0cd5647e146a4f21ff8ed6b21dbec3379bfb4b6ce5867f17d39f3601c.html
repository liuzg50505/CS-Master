Q2.3
Confidence reflects the frequency that the consequent occurs given the presence of the
antecedent; in other words, it is a conditional probability. Lift compares this confidence
to the baseline support of the consequent: if lift > 1, there is a positive association, lift = 1,
little or no real association, and lift < 1 is a negative association.
For the rule {Nail Polish,Bronzer}=>{Concealer}, confidence is relatively high, but a lift < 1
means that buying Nail Polish and Bronzer slightly decreases the likelihood of also
buying Concealer in comparison to the general population. Therefore, this rule is weak.
